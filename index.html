To be honest, when I first came to art practice in school I never asked myself what I am creating and why. It was all an expression of what I see, feel or interested in at that specific moment.
Studying design changed me and formed me in a new way. It helped me get my approaches, experiment as much as I can, ask questions to myself, fail and learn how to overcome that. For me personally, design is a cycle.
It often extends in the number of steps, but it is crucial to look back and see what you have done before. Observation, analysis and process is the key for me.
During the first year I mainly relied on the base that I had before - school. For instance, my biggest inspiration and influence (in my younger times) was Anselm Kiefer.
By looking at his work I unconsciously became open to large works and installations, realising it only now, by looking back. I was not afraid of going out and creating a piece with the materials I could find on the street.
It was all about the idea at that time and as long as it was there, I could easily spend hours, preparing for what I want to do.
